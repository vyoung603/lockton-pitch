# Mental Health Treatment Need Statistics - Citable Sources

## Executive Summary

**Key Finding**: Approximately 23% of US adults have any mental illness (AMI), but only 6% have serious mental illness (SMI) requiring intensive clinical intervention. Of those with AMI, only about 50% receive any treatment, and many could benefit from lighter-touch digital interventions rather than traditional therapy.

---

## 1. NIMH Statistics on Mental Illness Prevalence (2022)

**Source**: National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH), "Mental Illness" statistics page
**Data Year**: 2022 (from SAMHSA's National Survey on Drug Use and Health)
**URL**: https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/statistics/mental-illness

### Any Mental Illness (AMI)
- **59.3 million US adults** (23.1% of adult population) had Any Mental Illness in 2022
- AMI is defined as "a mental, behavioral, or emotional disorder" that can vary in impact from mild to severe
- Includes conditions ranging from mild anxiety to major depression

**Demographics**:
- Females: 26.4%
- Males: 19.7%
- Ages 18-25: 36.2%
- Ages 26-49: 29.4%
- Ages 50+: 13.9%

### Serious Mental Illness (SMI)
- **15.4 million US adults** (6.0% of adult population) had Serious Mental Illness in 2022
- SMI is defined as "a mental, behavioral, or emotional disorder resulting in serious functional impairment, which substantially interferes with or limits one or more major life activities"
- This is the subset requiring intensive clinical care

**Demographics**:
- Females: 7.1%
- Males: 4.8%
- Ages 18-25: 11.6%
- Ages 26-49: 7.6%
- Ages 50+: 3.0%

### The Gap: AMI vs SMI
- **~74% of people with mental illness have AMI but NOT SMI** (59.3M - 15.4M = 43.9M people)
- This represents the population with mild-to-moderate symptoms who may benefit from digital tools, coaching, self-care, or less intensive interventions

## 2. Treatment Access and the Care Gap

**Source**: Same NIMH/SAMHSA 2022 data

### Treatment Rates for AMI
- Of 59.3 million adults with AMI, only **30.0 million (50.6%) received any mental health treatment** in 2022
- This means **49.4% of people with mental illness received NO treatment**
- Treatment included inpatient care, outpatient counseling, or prescription medication

**By Gender**:
- Females with AMI receiving treatment: 56.9%
- Males with AMI receiving treatment: 41.6%

### Treatment Rates for SMI
- Of 15.4 million adults with SMI, **10.2 million (66.7%) received mental health treatment** in 2022
- This means **33.3% of people with SERIOUS mental illness still received no treatment**

**By Gender**:
- Females with SMI receiving treatment: 71.4%
- Males with SMI receiving treatment: 59.3%

## 3. WHO Global Statistics

**Source**: World Health Organization (WHO), "Mental disorders" fact sheet (updated September 2025)
**URL**: https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/mental-disorders

### Global Prevalence
- **Nearly 1 in 7 people (1.1 billion globally)** were living with a mental disorder in 2021
- Anxiety and depressive disorders are the most common
- **Anxiety disorders**: 359 million people globally (including 72 million children/adolescents)
- **Depression**: 280 million people globally in 2019 (including 23 million children/adolescents)
- **Schizophrenia**: ~23 million people (1 in 345 people worldwide)
- **Bipolar disorder**: 37 million people in 2021

### Global Treatment Gap
- **"Most people with mental disorders do not have access to effective care"** (WHO)
- Only **29% of people with psychosis** receive formal mental health care
- Only **one third of people with depression** receive formal mental health care

### Key WHO Statement
> "While effective prevention and treatment options exist, most people with mental disorders do not have access to effective care. Many people also experience stigma, discrimination and violations of human rights."

## 4. Implications for Digital Mental Health Solutions

### The 75/25 Hypothesis
Based on the NIMH data:
- **~74% of people with mental illness have AMI without SMI** (mild-to-moderate symptoms)
- **~26% have SMI** (serious functional impairment requiring intensive care)

### Treatment Modality Match
**For the ~74% with mild-to-moderate symptoms**:
- May benefit from digital tools, AI coaching, self-guided interventions
- Can include peer support, wellness programs, preventive care
- Lower cost, higher accessibility, reduced stigma
- Can serve as first-line intervention or complement to therapy

**For the ~26% with SMI**:
- Require licensed clinical care (therapy, psychiatry, medication management)
- May still benefit from digital tools as adjunct support
- Need higher touch, more intensive interventions

### The Unmet Need
- **49.4% of people with AMI receive NO treatment** (29.3 million people)
- This represents a massive opportunity for digital-first, scalable interventions
- Traditional therapy capacity cannot meet this demand

## Notes on Data Quality

### NIMH/SAMHSA Methodology
- Based on structured clinical interviews (SCID-I/NP adapted for DSM-IV-TR)
- Excludes institutionalized populations, active military, homeless without shelter
- 2022 data collection affected by COVID-19 (multimode: 40.7% web, 59.3% in-person)
- Overall response rate: 12.1% (due to pandemic conditions)
- Estimates may undercount true prevalence

### What's NOT Included
- Adult ADHD
- Autism spectrum disorders
- Full schizophrenia diagnosis (only psychotic symptom screen)
- Developmental disorders
- Substance use disorders

### Conservative Estimate
The 23.1% AMI prevalence is likely conservative given exclusions and non-response bias among people with mental illness.
